About Us
Our Mission Statement

Our Mission is to impart in the Students, Excellent Spiritual growth, excellent moral standard and excellent
academic performance which is geared towards building a total man. The school provides various platforms
for the spiritual development of the students and members of staff which are targeted at instilling love and fear of God,
discipline and enduring morals.

The Set-Up of R.P.S.

The school has a Secondary School pattern, but Seminary in outlook. It is a Comprehensive School.
It is Co-Educational i.e. The School admits both Boys and Girls.
It is a FULL BOARDING SCHOOL. The Schools Academic Programme is of a 6-year duration, of 3 years
each at the JUNIOR SECONDARY STAGE and at the SENIOR SECONDARY STAGE.
Both are run on the same premises with separate Head Teachers, after the full disarticulation of the school
with a priest as the Apical head.

History

The continuous need to improve the quality of Education at all levels of Education in Nigeria
has continued to engage the minds of all well meaning Nigerians.
This is because of the awareness of the significant contributions
which Education had made and can make to the five main objectives of Nigeria
as stated in the second National Development Plan and endorsed as the necessary foundation for the National Policy on Education.
These are:

  1. A free and democratic society
  2. A just and egalitarian society
  3. A united, strong and self-reliant nation
  4. A great and dynamic economy
  5. A land of bright and full opportunities for all citizens
In consequence, the quality of Education at all levels has to be oriented towards inculcating the following values:
  1. Respect for the worth and dignity of the individuals.
  2. Faith in Mens ability to make rational decisions.
  3. Moral and spiritual virtues in interpersonal and human relations
  4. Shared responsibility for the common good of the society
  5. Respect for the dignity of labour; and
  6. Promotion of the emotional, physical and psychological health of all children.
It was in view of the above, that the National policy on Education was published in 1977, revised in 1981 and 2004.
In that Policy, there is also a provision for the encouragement of Private Organisations and individual to establish
Private Secondary School after fulfilling and strictly adhering to certain conditions.

Following the take-over of both Primary and Secondary Schools by the Government of the Federation,
both at Federal and State levels in 1976 especially by the Lagos State Government,
religious organisations were no more in control of their schools which they shad once established.
The effect of this take-over of Schools was the fall in academic performance of students,
low moral standards and deteriorating physical infrastructural facilities in schools.
All efforts made by religious organisations for the government to return these schools to their original owners failed.

However, today, government has now come to terms with our public schools are no longer
the haven of academic excellence combined with sound moral ethics,
that the old mission owned schools used to be known for and stand for
. Government has therefore accepted in full, the need to allow, once again, private participation in Education,
through the establishment of private schools.
It is in line with this new posture and policy that the Diocese of Lagos (Anglican Communion),
under the able leadership of the Bishop of Lagos and the Archbishop of the Church of Nigeria (Anglican Communion),
the Late Most Revd. Dr. Joseph Abiodun Adetiloye, D.O., at the 2nd Session of the 25th Synod of the Diocese of Lagos
held in May 1993 at All Saints Church, Yaba, resolved to establish a new Secondary School which will be a model and of the standard required of a Mission School.
It was however made clear that, unlike in the past, the diocese of Lagos would make the School a JUNIOR SEMINARY,
which will not only incorporate the present system of Secondary School as put in the National Policy on Education but also include
certain curricular of the Seminary/Theological institution.
